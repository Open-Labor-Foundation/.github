# Contributing to the Open Labor Foundation

Thank you for your interest in contributing.

## Contribute your expertise

The foundation grows through people who know their field. If you have expertise in any profession, trade, or industry — and can describe what an AI specialist in that field should do, refuse, and know — that knowledge belongs in the catalog.

No engineering background required.

Use [commons-idea](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-idea) to describe your expertise through a guided conversation. It produces a structured definition you can submit as a pull request: specialist definitions to [labor-commons](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/labor-commons), written idea records to [commons-specs](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-specs), and working deliverables to [commons-artifacts](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/commons-artifacts). A streamlined in-product publishing flow is actively in development — this fork-and-PR path is how it works today.

## Code and infrastructure contributions

The repositories that make up the platform's engineering — `commons-devloop`, `commons-board`, `commons-crew`, `commons-idea`, `commons-keeper`, `open-labor-foundation`, `foundation-site` — are not currently accepting external issues or pull requests. Issues are disabled organization-wide, and PRs from outside collaborators aren't reviewed at this stage. This is expected to change as the project matures — check back, or watch [openlabor.foundation](https://openlabor.foundation) for updates.

## Code of conduct

All contributors are expected to follow the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
